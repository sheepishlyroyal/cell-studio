01 · THE CENTRAL DOGMA

Information flows from DNA to RNA to Protein.

Every cell in your body runs on the same principle, first proposed by Francis Crick in 1958. DNA is the archive. RNA is the messenger. Protein is the machine that does the work.

In this game you will rebuild that pipeline with your own hands — matching base pairs, landing an RNA polymerase, guiding mRNA through the cytoplasm, and decoding codons into amino acids.

02 · LEVEL 1 — DNA BUILDING

Two strands. One zipper of hydrogen bonds.

DNA is a double helix. Each rung of the ladder is a pair of bases held together by hydrogen bonds — A always pairs with T, and C always pairs with G. This simple rule is how genetic information is copied faithfully every time a cell divides.

In Level 1, a template strand appears on the left. Your job is to place the complementary base on the right. Three mutations and the strand collapses — your cell takes damage.

  • 3.2B base pairs in the human genome
  • 2m of DNA coiled inside each cell
  • 99.9% of DNA is identical between any two humans

Hover the pairs — they flash their hydrogen bonds.

03 · LEVEL 2 — TRANSCRIPTION

Copying DNA into a portable message.

PROMOTER

Press any key when the droplet is over the promoter.

DNA never leaves the nucleus — it's too precious. Instead, an enzyme called RNA polymerase binds to a special region called the promoter, unzips the double helix, and copies one strand into a messenger molecule called mRNA.

In Level 2, the RNA polymerase droplet bounces across the screen. Land it on the promoter three times to finish transcription. Miss too often and the cell shuts transcription down.

04 · LEVEL 3 — mRNA MAZE

A dangerous journey through the cytoplasm.

Once the mRNA is made, it has to travel from the nucleus, out through a nuclear pore, and across the cytoplasm to a ribosome. Along the way it faces hungry enzymes (RNases) that can shred it, and it needs ATP energy to keep moving.

In Level 3 you steer the mRNA through a maze. Dodge enzyme hazards, collect glowing energy packets, and reach the ribosome before your timer — or your health — runs out.

The ribosome reads mRNA in groups of three nucleotides called codons. Each codon specifies exactly one amino acid. String enough amino acids together and you have a protein — the molecular machines that run every process in your body.

06 · THE CELL HEALTH BAR

Every mistake has a biological cost.

CELL HEALTH: 100%

Mutations, failed transcription, degraded mRNA, misfolded proteins — each one reduces your cell's health. Hit zero and the cell dies. The bar unifies all four levels into one story.
