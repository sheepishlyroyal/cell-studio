STEP 01

Replication

Before a cell divides, every strand of DNA is copied — letter for letter — into two identical helices.

STEP 02

Transcription

RNA polymerase opens the helix at a promoter and transcribes one strand into messenger RNA.

STEP 03

Transport

The mRNA leaves the nucleus through a pore and travels through the cytoplasm to a ribosome.

STEP 04

Translation

The ribosome reads codons three letters at a time, assembling amino acids into a finished protein.

01 · THE CENTRAL DOGMA

Information flows from DNA to RNA to Protein.

First described by Francis Crick in 1958, the central dogma is the blueprint every living cell follows. DNA is the archive. RNA is the messenger. Protein is the machine that gets the work done.

In this game you rebuild that pipeline by hand — matching base pairs, landing an RNA polymerase, guiding mRNA through the cytoplasm, and decoding codons into amino acids.

02 · LEVEL 1 — DNA BUILDING

Two strands. One zipper of hydrogen bonds.

DNA is a double helix. Each rung of the ladder is a pair of bases held together by hydrogen bonds — A always pairs with T, and C always pairs with G. This rule is how genetic information is copied faithfully every time a cell divides.

In Level 1, a template strand appears on the left. Your job is to place the complementary base on the right. Three mutations and the strand collapses — your cell takes damage.

03 · LEVEL 2 — TRANSCRIPTION

Copy the code into a portable message.

PROMOTER

Click anywhere in the track when the droplet is over the promoter.

DNA never leaves the nucleus — it's too precious. Instead, RNA polymerase binds to a region called the promoter, unzips the double helix, and copies one strand into messenger RNA.

In Level 2, the polymerase droplet bounces across the screen. Land it on the promoter three times to finish transcription. Miss too often and the cell shuts transcription down.

04 · LEVEL 3 — mRNA MAZE

Reach the ribosome before you degrade.

Once mRNA is made, it has to travel from the nucleus, out through a nuclear pore, and across the cytoplasm to a ribosome. Hungry enzymes (RNases) can shred it on the way, and it needs ATP energy to keep moving.

In Level 3 you steer the mRNA through a maze. Dodge enzymes, collect glowing energy packets, and reach the ribosome before your timer — or your cell health — runs out.

05 · LEVEL 4 — TRANSLATION

Read the code, three letters at a time.

The ribosome reads mRNA in groups of three nucleotides called codons. Each codon specifies exactly one amino acid. Chain enough amino acids together and you have a protein — the molecular machines that run every process in your body.

06 · THE CELL HEALTH BAR

One unified stakes system across all four levels.

CELL HEALTH: 100%

Mutations, failed transcription, degraded mRNA, and misfolded proteins all drain the same cell-health bar. Hit zero and the cell dies.
